01 PRESSED WOOL / RAW SILK

A study in architectural softness, this piece centers on the juxtaposition of structured wool and ethereal silk. The internal framework is entirely hand-canvassed, allowing the garment to hold its silhouette while responding to the wearer’s movement. Each seam is bound in bias-cut silk for a seamless finish.

A transition into fluid weightlessness. This silhouette utilizes bias-cut sand-washed silk to create a garment that flows like liquid against the form. The internal structure is reduced to minimalist silk-taped seams, emphasizing a sense of unburdened luxury and organic movement.

03 ARCHITECTURAL TWEED / SILK ORGANZA

A dialogue between weight and transparency. This look features a hand-woven architectural tweed jacket paired with a floor-length silk organza skirt. The raw edges of the tweed are deliberately exposed to celebrate the artisanal process, while the organza is layered in triple-tiers to create a ghostly, structural volume.

An exploration of sculptural volume and internal support. This look utilizes heavy-bonded crepe to create a rigid, defiant silhouette that stands away from the body. The intricate pleating at the waist is engineered to distribute weight evenly, while the hidden corset structure ensures a sharp, architectural finish that requires no external fastening.
